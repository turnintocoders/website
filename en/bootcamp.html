Bootcamp

The bootcamp is a super intensive training course, in a limited amount of time, and when you are done you've learnt a job and you can dive in the market of web developers.

8 intensive weeks

The heart of the bootcamp is formed by 8 intensive weeks in which you'll attend workshops and build projects, everything in 4 groups of 4 people, always in a cooperative way.

During the course you'll be exposed to test-driven development (TDD), full-stack development in JavaScript (and Node.js), relational databases. We'll also touch concepts of UX design and project management.

You can find more details about the content of the bootcamp in this Github repository.

Projects & mentorship

Once the bootcamp is over you can test yourself developing a project for 4 weeks, followed by 8 weeks of mentorship, in which you'll have the opportunity of creating your own curriculum building projects, meeting companies or creating your own startup!

Prerequisites

Before attending the bootcamp you need to have followed a path on your own. That will be useful also in understanding if you are really interested in becoming a web developer and it will allow you to reach a skill level that will be as omogeneous as possible with the other students.

The prerequisites are:

  1. Register on Github
  2. Join our chat on Gitter and actively participate
  3. Register on FreeCodeCamp and complete the sections:
    • HTML5 & CSS
    • Basic JavaScript
    • Object Oriented and Functional Programming
    • Basic Algorithm Scripting
  4. When you are confident enought, build a one page website using Github pages
  5. Register on Codewars and, in JavaScript:
    • Reach 5th kyu
    • Create one kata

To avoid making the achievements of the prerequisites too frustrating or long, you can join us at the weekly meetups where we can help you finishing them.

Another form of support and preparation for the bootcamp are mini-bootcamps, each one lasting one week.
